# About me

Hi, I’m Ruoyu Niu.

I am a postdoctoral researcher interested in inter-brain sensorimotor synchronization and learning. My work focuses on how coordinated behavior unfolds over time, especially the dynamic balance between intra-individual processing and inter-individual coupling. I mainly use fNIRS to study fine-grained temporal dynamics in social interaction.

Outside the lab, I enjoy R&B, J-pop, badminton, and the occasional wander through virtual worlds. Overall, I really love what I do.


## 🔬 Research Interests

I am broadly interested in how coordinated behavior emerges, changes, and stabilizes across time. My research combines behavioral paradigms and neuroimaging methods to investigate the dynamic mechanisms underlying human synchronization and learning. My core interests include:

- **Sensorimotor Synchronization and Social Learning**  
  I explore how people synchronize their actions with rhythmic stimuli and with other individuals, and how these processes support adaptation, coordination, and learning in social settings.

- **Dynamic Functional Connectivity Using Sliding-Window Approaches**  
  I examine how functional brain networks reorganize over time, with a focus on dynamic connectivity patterns captured through sliding-window analysis.

## 💻 Skills

I use a combination of neuroimaging methods and computational tools in my research. My main technical skills include:

- **Neuroimaging**: fNIRS, EEG  
- **Programming and Software**: MATLAB, Python
